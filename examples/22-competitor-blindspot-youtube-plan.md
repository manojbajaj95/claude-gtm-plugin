# The Competitor-Blindspot YouTube Plan

Find the sub-topic your top 3 niche competitors have ignored or botched on YouTube, then build a complete 10-minute video outline and three high-retention hooks designed to own that gap.

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Audit competitors for content gaps

```
Conduct a competitor analysis on the top 3 YouTube channels in our niche.
Channels to analyze: [Competitor Channel 1], [Competitor Channel 2], [Competitor Channel 3]

For each channel review:
  - Their 20 most-viewed videos (title, topic, view count)
  - Their comment sections: what questions are going unanswered?
  - Their low-performing videos: which topics underperformed relative to their average?
  - Topics their audience asks for in comments that the channel never covers

Identify one specific sub-topic that meets both criteria:
  - Has clear audience demand (people are asking for it)
  - Has been either ignored entirely or covered poorly (short, vague, or outdated)

Return: the gap sub-topic, evidence of demand, and why existing coverage falls short.
```

### Step 2 — Plan the video

```
Using the gap sub-topic from Step 1, outline a 10-minute YouTube video that fills
this knowledge gap completely.

Read strategy/brand.md for our angle, audience, and voice.

Deliver:
  - Video title: 3 options (SEO-optimized, under 60 characters, front-load the keyword)
  - Target keyword: primary search term this video should rank for
  - Thumbnail concept: describe the visual composition and any text overlay
    (what makes someone click this over a competitor's)
  - Full script outline:
      0:00–0:30: Hook (covered in Step 3)
      0:30–1:30: Problem framing — why this question matters and why others get it wrong
      1:30–8:30: Core content — divide into 3–4 clearly named chapters with H2 headings
                 and bullet points for each sub-point to cover
      8:30–9:30: Summary — the 3 things viewers now know that they didn't before
      9:30–10:00: CTA — subscribe prompt, related video suggestion, comment question
  - Chapter timestamps (YouTube description format)
```

### Step 3 — Write 3 high-retention hooks

```
Write 3 distinct opening hooks (0:00–0:30) for the video planned in Step 2.
Each hook should be designed to prevent the specific exit patterns most common in
educational YouTube content (losing viewers before the payoff is established).

Hook structures to use:
  Hook 1 — The Counterintuitive Claim:
    Open with a statement that contradicts conventional wisdom on the topic.
    Follow immediately with "Here's why — and what to do instead."

  Hook 2 — The Specific Failure Scenario:
    Open in the middle of a recognizable painful moment.
    Establish stakes before naming the video's topic.

  Hook 3 — The Proof-First Hook:
    Open with the end result or outcome first.
    Then rewind: "But to get there, here's what most people miss."

Each hook: 75–100 words, written as a spoken script (natural cadence, no bullet points).
End each with a retention line that commits the viewer to staying:
("Stay with me for 90 seconds — this changes how you think about [topic].")
```

## Output

- `content/youtube/drafts/YYYY-MM-DD_video-plan-<topic-slug>.md` — full video outline with title options and thumbnail concept
- `content/youtube/drafts/YYYY-MM-DD_hooks-<topic-slug>.md` — 3 opening hooks
