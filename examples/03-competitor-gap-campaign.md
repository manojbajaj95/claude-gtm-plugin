# The Competitor Gap Campaign

Turn a competitor's weakness — surfaced from real user complaints — into a targeted ad campaign that positions your product's ROI without naming names.

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Find the complaint gap

```
Competitor: [Competitor Name]
Focus: their latest pricing change or major feature update
Sources to scan: G2/Capterra reviews, Reddit threads, Twitter/X mentions,
app store reviews (published in the last 60 days)
Goal: Identify the single complaint that appears most frequently. Return:
  - The complaint verbatim (or paraphrased from multiple sources)
  - Why users feel this way (root cause)
  - Which user segment complains most
```

### Step 2 — Build the counter-narrative

```
Read strategy/brand.md for our current positioning and key differentiators.
Complaint to address: [output from Step 1]
Goal: Draft a counter-narrative that:
  - Centers on ROI and measurable user outcomes (not feature lists)
  - Implicitly addresses the competitor's gap without naming them
  - Aligns with our existing brand voice and ICP
Return: a 2–3 sentence core message and 3 supporting proof points.
```

### Step 3 — Write ad copy

```
Core message: [output from Step 2]
Platforms: Meta (Facebook/Instagram) and LinkedIn
Deliver 3 ad copy variations, each with:
  - Headline (under 40 characters)
  - Primary text (under 125 characters for Meta / under 150 for LinkedIn)
  - CTA button text
Variation angles:
  1. Outcome-focused (what they gain)
  2. Pain-point relief (what they escape)
  3. Social proof (what others like them achieved)
Do NOT name the competitor in any variation.
```

## Output

- A counter-narrative brief saved to `strategy/brand.md` (append under a "Campaign Messaging" section)
- `content/ads/drafts/YYYY-MM-DD_competitor-gap-<slug>.md` — 3 ad copy sets
