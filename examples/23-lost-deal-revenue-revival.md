# The "Lost Deal" Revenue Revival

Dig up closed-lost leads who cited budget as their reason for leaving, segment them by company size, and win them back with a targeted campaign built around an exclusive, time-limited entry tier.

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Audit closed-lost leads

```
Dive into our CRM to audit all closed-lost leads from the past 6 months.
Filter criteria:
  - Status: closed-lost
  - Close date: within the last 6 months
  - Lost reason: "budget", "too expensive", "not in budget", "price", or equivalent tag

For the filtered list return:
  - Total count of budget-related closed-lost leads
  - Breakdown by lost reason phrasing (some may say "budget now, revisit later")
  - Distribution across deal stages (early discovery vs. late negotiation)
  - Any notes logged about when they might revisit
```

### Step 2 — Segment by company size

```
Using the closed-lost list from Step 1, segment these leads by company size.
Use the following tiers (adjust if our CRM uses different fields):
  - Micro: 1–10 employees
  - Small: 11–50 employees
  - Mid-market: 51–200 employees
  - Enterprise: 201+ employees

For each segment return:
  - Count of leads
  - Average deal size they were quoted
  - Most common objection detail (e.g., "full plan was too expensive" vs. "any cost is too high right now")
  - Recommended re-engagement priority (highest potential ROI segment first)
```

### Step 3 — Design the bootstrap tier offer

```
Read strategy/brand.md for our current pricing tiers and product positioning.
Design an exclusive, limited-time "bootstrap" entry tier to offer these closed-lost accounts.

The tier should:
  - Remove the primary budget barrier (lower entry price, stripped-back but genuinely useful)
  - Have a clear upgrade path built in (not a dead-end plan)
  - Feel exclusive to this group, not like a public discount (frame as early-access or alumni pricing)
  - Have a hard expiry date to create honest urgency (suggested: 14 days from outreach)

Deliver:
  - Tier name and price point
  - What's included (3–5 features or capabilities — the ones most valuable to early-stage users)
  - What's excluded (be transparent)
  - The upgrade trigger: what behavior or usage signals readiness to move up
  - Suggested expiry date based on today's date
```

### Step 4 — Build the re-engagement campaign

```
Using the segmentation from Step 2 and the offer from Step 3, create a targeted
lead generation and demand campaign for these closed-lost accounts.

Read strategy/brand.md for positioning and ICP.

Deliver:

  Outreach email (personalized per segment):
    - Subject line: reference the prior conversation without being awkward
      ("We've been thinking about what you said...")
    - Opening: acknowledge the time gap, no groveling
    - Body: one sentence on what's changed since they left (product update or new tier)
      + 2–3 sentences on the bootstrap tier and its specific value for their company size
    - CTA: single link to a dedicated landing page for this offer (no generic homepage)
    - Under 150 words
    - Write one version for Micro/Small and one for Mid-market segments

  LinkedIn outreach message (connection or InMail):
    - Under 300 characters
    - Reference the prior interest, tease the offer, ask for a quick reply — not a call booking

  Campaign parameters:
    - Recommended send window (day of week + time)
    - Batch order: start with highest-priority segment from Step 2
    - Success metric: reply rate (not open rate) as primary KPI
    - Follow-up rule: one follow-up only, 5 days after first touch, then close the loop
```

## Output

- `content/briefs/drafts/YYYY-MM-DD_lost-deal-revival-brief.md` — tier design and segmentation summary
- `content/email/drafts/YYYY-MM-DD_lost-deal-outreach-sequence.md` — email and LinkedIn copy for each segment
