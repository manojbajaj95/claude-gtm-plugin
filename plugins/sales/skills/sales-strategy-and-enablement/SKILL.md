# Sales Strategy & Enablement

Comprehensive sales playbook covering team building, deal execution, and sales enablement for B2B companies at all stages.

## Quick Start

1. **Assess Stage** — Founder-led, early, or scaling?
2. **Define Motion** — Inbound, outbound, PLG, or hybrid?
3. **Build Process** — Discovery, demo, close
4. **Create Collateral** — Decks, one-pagers, scripts
5. **Qualify Rigorously** — Focus on fit, not volume

## Founder-Led Sales

### Core Principles

**The founder IS the product**
> "Founder-led sales is really that first milestone... the founder is the product." — Jen Abel

**Your biggest competitor is indecision**
> "40 to 60% of B2B purchase processes end in no decision." — April Dunford

**You cannot outsource early sales**
> "The founder's gotta do that stuff... it's gonna be way easier for you to get minimally viable good at selling." — Pete Kazanjy

### Key Tactics

- **Sell before you build** — Use Figma mockups to secure first customers
- **Close the laptop** — Focus on diagnosis, not demos
- **Collison Install** — Don't leave until implemented
- **Book next meeting on this one** — Maintain momentum
- **Manual outreach** — Personalized beats automated for high-value deals

### When to Hire

> "Wait until you're around a million in ARR. When you have a repeatable process." — Jeanne Grosser

## Building Sales Team

### Core Principles

**Prove repeatability before hiring**
> "You can reliably, at a pretty okay win rate, so maybe 15% or 20% or 25%, turn first meetings into eventual customers." — Pete Kazanjy

**Always hire in pairs**
> "You need to hire one rep and you've got to hire two because otherwise, there's no A-B test." — Jason Lemkin

**Delay sales hires until Series A**
> "Seed is all about experimentation... Series A is about exploiting that learning." — Jen Abel

**Match profiles to motion**
> "In those early days... the type of sales reps you might need are not necessarily going to be outbound." — Annie Pearl

### Hiring Framework

| Stage | Who to Hire | When |
|-------|-------------|------|
| Founder-led | 0 | Before $1M ARR |
| First AEs | 2 reps | After proven repeatability |
| SDRs | 1-2 | When inbound Overflows |
| VP Sales | 1 | When 2+ reps hitting quota |
| Managers | 1 per 5 reps | At scale |

### Don't Hire VP Too Early
> "You need two sales reps hitting quota closing deals before you're ready to hire a manager." — Jason Lemkin

## Enterprise Sales

### Core Principles

**40-60% of deals die to indecision**
> "Most deals are lost not to competitors, but to customer inertia and fear." — Matt Dixon

**Identify and arm the champion**
> "The champion's job is to get consensus and champion the deal across everybody." — April Dunford

**FOMU beats FOMO**
> "People don't want to be blamed for making decisions that lead to a loss." — Matt Dixon

**Make procurement's job easy**
> "When you get to procurement, you're going to have to do all the work. Make their job easy." — Jen Abel

### Buying Committee Mapping

| Role | Responsibility | Key Concern |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| Champion | Internal advocate | Career risk |
| Economic Buyer | Signs the check | ROI, risk |
| Technical Buyer | Evaluates product | Security, integration |
| End User | Uses daily | Ease, workflow |
| Blocker | Can say no | Unknown |

### The JOLT Method

1. **Judge** — Level of indecision
2. **Offer** — Firm recommendation
3. **Limit** — Exploration by building trust
4. **Take risk off the table** — De-risk the deal

## Product-Led Sales

### Core Principles

**PLS converts usage into sales opportunities**
> "Product-led sales converts the usage that you've generated via self-serve into a sales opportunity." — Elena Verna

**Use data signals for outreach**
> "Our sales team get that data signal, they may send an email and reach out." — Hila Qu

**Sales should help, not sell**
> The initial outreach should offer assistance based on observed behavior.

### PLS Triggers

- Usage threshold reached
- Feature adoption spike
- Team expansion
- Enterprise features requested
- Competitor mention in product

### When to Transition

- Self-serve ceiling (typically $10K-$15K)
- User needs capabilities beyond self-serve tier
- Multiple seats needed

## Sales Qualification

### Core Principles

**Most sales problems are qualification problems**
> "If you spend your time on the wrong leads, that equates to a zero." — Jen Abel

**"No" is a successful outcome**
> "On the first call it's either a yes or a no, there's no in between."

**Disqualify aggressively**
> Better to pursue fewer, better-qualified opportunities.

### Qualification Frameworks

**BANT**
- Budget
- Authority
- Need
- Timeline

**MEDDIC**
- Metrics
- Economic Buyer
- Decision Criteria
- Decision Process
- Identify Pain
- Champion

### Discovery Questions

- What percentage of pipeline actually closes?
- What are characteristics of best customers?
- What questions reveal qualification?
- When was last disqualification on first call?

## Sales Enablement

### Sales Deck Framework (10-12 Slides)

1. **Current World Problem** — The pain
2. **Cost of the Problem** — Inaction costs
3. **The Shift Happening** — Market urgency
4. **Your Approach** — Your solution
5. **Product Walkthrough** — 3-4 key workflows
6. **Proof Points** — Metrics, logos
7. **Case Study** — One story well-told
8. **Implementation** — Timeline
9. **ROI/Value** — Expected return
10. **Pricing Overview** — Transparent
11. **Next Steps/CTA** — Clear action

### One-Pager Structure

1. Problem statement (1 sentence)
2. Your solution (how)
3. 3 differentiators
4. Proof point (1 metric/quote)
5. CTA with contact info

### Objection Handling

| Category | Examples | Response Approach |
|----------|----------|-------------------|
| Price | "Too expensive" | ROI angle, payment terms |
| Timing | "Not right time" | Urgency, cost of delay |
| Competition | "Already use X" | Differentiation, unique value |
| Authority | "Need to check with boss" | Arm the champion |
| Status quo | "Works fine" | Cost of inaction |
| Technical | "Security concerns" | Documentation, certifications |

### Demo Script Structure

1. **Opening** (2 min) — Context, agenda, confirm goals
2. **Discovery Recap** (3 min) — Summarize what you learned
3. **Solution Walkthrough** (15-20 min) — 3-4 workflows mapped to pain
4. **Interaction** — Questions during, not just at end
5. **Close** (5 min) — Summarize value, propose next steps

## Sales Best Practices

### Do

1. Qualify ruthlessly — "No" is a win
2. Focus on decision-maker — Don't waste time elsewhere
3. Build champion — Arm them to sell internally
4. Address FOMU — Not just FOMO
5. Document process — Make it repeatable
6. Hire in pairs — Test the motion
7. Prove repeatability — Before scaling

### Don't

1. Hire before proving the motion
2. Default to demos — Diagnose first
3. Treat "yes" as finish — Implement matters
4. Use generic AI outreach — Personalization wins
5. Chase polite leads — "Sounds interesting" ≠ intent
6. Ignore procurement — Make it easy

## Common Mistakes

### Founder Sales Mistakes

- Hiring sales too early
- Defaulting to product demos
- Treating "yes" as finish line
- Using generic AI outreach
- Chasing polite leads

### Team Building Mistakes

- Hiring before repeatability
- Hiring VP of Sales first
- Mismatching profile to motion
- Single-point-of-failure hiring
- Ignoring 50% failure rate

### Enterprise Mistakes

- Relying on FOMO alone
- Ignoring the champion
- Passive procurement handling
- Demo-focused POCs
- Best-case projections
